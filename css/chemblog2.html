Preparation of Cuprous Bromide(CuBr)

Sr.No Compound Molecular Weight Quantity Moles Equivalent
1. CuSo4.5H2O 249.64 20 g 0.080 1
2. NaBr 102.90 3.861 g 0.0375 0.47
3. NaOH 40 2.4 g 0.06 0.76
4. NaHSO3 104.69 5.234 g 0.05 0.63
5. DM Water 60 ml 3
6. DM Water 40 ml 2

Preparation:

  1. A 4-necked round bottomed flask (250 ml) was taken.
  2. CuSO4 5H2O (20g, 0.080 mole) was taken in the flask. Demineralized water (DM) (60 ml) was added. The reaction mixture was stirred to make the solution homogeneous.
  3. The reaction mixture was stirred for 20 minutes at room temperature.
  4. NaBr (3.861g, 0.0375 mole) was added to the reaction mixture.
  5. In another flask, a solution of NaOH (2.4g, 0.060 mole) in water (60 ml) was prepared. NaHSO3 (5.234g, 0.05 mole)) was added and the aqueous solution thus obtained was transferred to the dropping funnel. The solution was added drop by drop to the reaction mass through the dropping funnel.
  6. On addition, an off-white solid precipitated out of the solution.
  7. The solid was then filtered, washed with DM water (200 ml), Methanol (80 ml), Ethyl acetate (50 ml) and Hexane (100 ml) and air dried. The solid thus obtained was used in Williamsonâ€™s ether synthesis as described under.
