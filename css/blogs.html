Ohm's law

The mathematical statement of Ohm’s law is expressed as:

V=IR

In this equation, V is the potential difference between the surfaces of a conductor, and I the current across those surfaces, implying the direction of the flow of current is perpendicular to either of the surfaces. R is a proportionality constant called resistance.

The resistance R of a conductor is related quantitatively to its dimensions and the type of material in it. The dimension means its length and the cross sectional area. The dimensions and the material content of a conductor are independent to each other, and therefore, in mathematical language, may be considered independent variables; and R, being dependent on both the variables, is the dependent variable.

To arrive at the function connecting the variables, I called upon the basic concepts of electricity I have learnt in my grade 12. I discovered that the act of connecting a conductor to a battery creates two equipotential surfaces, sandwiching a uniform electric field. The constancy of the electric field is a consequence of the concept of electric field lines. The lines in this case are straight lines and parallel to each other, running perpendicular to the surfaces. This led me to get at the function given below:

R = ρ × L/A

Here L is the distance between the equipotential surfaces, or the length of the conductor and A is surface area. The proportionality constant ρ, called resistivity, represents the material content of the conductor. The conductors are distinguished from each other by this constant.

Derivables from the Ohm’s Law:

  1. 1. The electric wires carrying currents to our homes run hundreds of kilometers from the power stations. The electric field in the wire between two stations must be constant.
  2. 2. The gravitational field lines, unlike electric field lines, do not terminate. These span out through the gigantic interstellar space to rule over our earth and the heaven.
  3. 3. The analysis aligns with a well established method in physics- separation of variables. The independent variables of a conductor are separated to be multiplied to give its resistance.
  4. 4. The Ohm’s law may be related to other phenomena in nature. The analogies are narrated in the table below.

Table

S.No Ohm’s Law Analogy
1 The flow of something (electrons) between two equipotential surfaces through a uniform field (electric). The flow of something (raindrops) in a uniform gravitational field. As compared to the radius of the earth, the distance of this imaginary spherical shell from the surface is negligible. Therefore, the gravitational field at any point in the intervening space may be considered to be a constant.

The electric permittivity and magnetic permittivity are two independent properties of free space. Therefore, both the variables must be multiplied to obtain the determining factor of the flow of something (electromagnetic wave) through the space.
